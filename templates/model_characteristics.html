Tuning the model


Our model utilizes a convolutional neural network to learn and recognize brick homes. A convolutional neural network (CNN/ConvNet) learns weights and biases from an input image. CNNs have become dominant in various deep learning and computer vision tasks due to their ability to quickly learn spatial hierarchies.


Convolutional neural networks are designed for working with two-dimensional image data, which our model utilizes. Convolution accounts for nearby pixels by multiplying a pixel and its neighboring pixelâ€™s color value by a matrix, tensor, or vector. In the development of our model, we were concerned with overfitting and underfitting. Overfitting occurs when the model is unable to generalize, while an underfit model is too inflexible to account for new and unseen data.
